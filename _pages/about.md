***Angelo M. Andriano*** is a PhD Student at IULM University in the field of the ***Philosophy of Games***, ***Game Studies*** and ***Philosophy of Technology***. 

He is developing a project about the ways in which videogames can decenter the players to foster systemtic and environmental awareness. 

He is interested in the themes of **interactive irony**, **game rules**, **systemic design** and how videogames leverage *aesthetics* of friction to promote rich experiences for players beyond mere fun and entertainment. 

His works have appeared on ***Games and Culture***, ***Eludamos*** and forthcoming in _Scholar's Codex_, a book on academics' perspectives about Soulslike games, published by Tune and Fairweather.
